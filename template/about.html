About This Project

Learn about our methodology, technology, and the importance of combating misinformation.

What is Fake News?

Fake news refers to false or misleading information presented as news. It often has the aim of damaging the reputation of a person or entity, or making money through advertising revenue. Unlike news satire, fake news tries to mislead, rather than entertain readers for humorous purposes.

How Our Detector Works

Our fake news detector uses machine learning to analyze text content and identify patterns associated with misleading or fabricated news. The system makes predictions based on:

  • Linguistic patterns and writing style
  • Use of sensationalist language
  • Context and content inconsistencies
  • Historical patterns from known fake vs. real news

Our Machine Learning Model

We use a Logistic Regression model trained on thousands of labeled news articles from reliable datasets. The model analyzes text features through:

  • Text preprocessing (tokenization, stop word removal, lemmatization)
  • TF-IDF vectorization to identify significant word patterns
  • Probability calibration for confidence scoring
  • Regular retraining to adapt to evolving news patterns

Performance and Accuracy

Our model achieves approximately 90-95% accuracy on test datasets. However, we emphasize that no automated system is perfect, and results should be interpreted as probabilistic rather than definitive. False positives and false negatives may still occur.

For best results, use this tool as part of a broader fact-checking approach that includes:

  • Consulting multiple news sources
  • Checking the author's credibility
  • Looking for original sources in articles
  • Being skeptical of extraordinary claims

Privacy and Data Usage

When you use our detector:

  • We do not store the text you submit for analysis
  • Analysis is performed locally on our server and results are immediately returned
  • No personal information is collected beyond basic usage analytics

Technology Stack

This project is built using:

  • Python and Flask for the backend
  • Scikit-learn for machine learning
  • NLTK for natural language processing
  • HTML/CSS/JavaScript for the frontend
